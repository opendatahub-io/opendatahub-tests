# TC-E2E-002: Job queuing under resource pressure — submit, wait, admit, complete

**Objective**: Verify the end-to-end flow when a job must wait in the queue due to resource pressure and is admitted once resources are freed.

**Preconditions**:

- Kueue Operator installed on the cluster
- ClusterQueue `eval-cq` created with nominalQuota: cpu=500m, memory=1Gi (deliberately small)
- LocalQueue `eval-queue` created in test namespace mapped to `eval-cq`
- EvalHub deployed and accessible

**Test Steps**:

1. **Setup**: Create ClusterQueue with small quota (cpu=500m, memory=1Gi), create LocalQueue
2. **Submit job-a**: POST /api/v1/evaluations/jobs requesting cpu=500m, memory=1Gi — consumes full quota
3. **Verify job-a admitted**: Workload shows `Admitted=True`, EvalHub reports `running`
4. **Submit job-b**: POST /api/v1/evaluations/jobs requesting cpu=500m, memory=1Gi
5. **Verify job-b pending**: EvalHub reports `pending`, Workload shows `Admitted=False`
6. **Verify LocalQueue**: Shows 1 pending, 1 admitted
7. **Free quota**: Cancel job-a via DELETE /api/v1/evaluations/jobs/{job-a-id}
8. **Verify auto-admission**: Job-b transitions from pending to running within 60 seconds
9. **Verify job-b Workload**: Shows `Admitted=True`, `QuotaReserved=True`
10. **Wait for completion**: Job-b completes (or cancel after verifying admission)
11. **Teardown**: Delete all jobs, LocalQueue, and ClusterQueue

**Expected Results**:

- Job-a is admitted immediately, consuming full quota
- Job-b remains pending while job-a is running
- After job-a cancellation, job-b is automatically admitted without manual intervention
- The complete flow demonstrates: submit → queue → wait → auto-admit → run → complete
- LocalQueue status accurately reflects pending and admitted counts throughout
